Disclosure principles

Make the relationship
easy to understand.

Future affiliate content should help a buyer understand when OMNI TERRAIN has a financial or other material connection to a product link, while keeping the recommendation information useful and specific.

Clear placement01

Place the disclosure where the decision happens.

The disclosure should be visible with the recommendation or link that creates the connection—not separated from the recommendation by a long page, a vague footer or a hidden policy link.

01
Near the recommendation

Show the affiliate relationship before or alongside the relevant product link, comparison or call to action.

02
Plain language

Use words a buyer can understand, such as commission, paid link or affiliate link—not a vague label alone.

03
Easy to notice

Do not rely on small text, a distant footer or a separate page as the only disclosure for a specific recommendation.

Pre-launch note: The final disclosure language must be checked against each actual page layout, link placement, device view and affiliate-program requirement.
Editorial integrity02

Do not turn a commission into a false claim.

Affiliate compensation should not change the standard for factual product information. Any future recommendation needs clear sourcing, realistic limitations and claims that are supported by the product information available.

01
No invented experience

Do not imply that OMNI TERRAIN tested, owned or used a product unless that is actually true and can be described honestly.

02
No fake endorsement

Do not publish false reviews, fabricated customer outcomes or a guaranteed performance claim for a product.

03
State relevant limits

Fitment, battery capacity, vehicle setup, marine system and installation requirements should not be hidden to make a recommendation look broader than it is.

Pre-launch note: Buyer-guide content and product claims should be reviewed for accuracy before each affiliate campaign or recommendation is published.
Third-party purchase03

The merchant controls the third-party sale.

Where a buyer follows a future affiliate link to another merchant, the merchant’s pricing, availability, shipping, returns, privacy practices and checkout terms apply to that purchase. OMNI TERRAIN should make this distinction clear where it matters.

01
Check the merchant page

Prices, product availability and merchant policies can change, so the buyer should confirm the final terms with the seller before purchase.

02
Separate support paths

OMNI TERRAIN may assist with a recommendation question, but a merchant order, delivery or return is generally handled through the relevant seller process.

03
Do not overstate control

Do not promise delivery, returns, warranties or stock status for a merchant sale that OMNI TERRAIN does not control.

Pre-launch note: The final page should name or link to specific affiliate-program or merchant terms only where those terms are current and applicable.

Activation checklist

What happens
before the first link.

Affiliate content should go live only when the real program, merchant terms, disclosure wording, link tracking and buyer guidance are documented. The disclosure must match what the page actually does.

1Confirm acceptance into the relevant affiliate program and read its current content, trademark and disclosure requirements.
2Place a specific plain-language disclosure immediately next to each future affiliate link or recommendation section.
3Verify the merchant page, product context, compatibility information and any claims in the buyer guide before publication.
4Update privacy, cookie and terms disclosures if affiliate tracking, analytics or third-party redirects add new data flows.
5Review the final disclosure with counsel before commercial affiliate content launches.
