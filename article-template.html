The Beginner's Guide to 48V Solar Setup

Why everyone is upgrading from 12V to 48V, and exactly how you can do it safely to run your fridge, AC, and lights.

The Problem with Old 12V Systems

For a long time, if you wanted power in an RV or cabin, you hooked up a bunch of 12V batteries. The problem? If you want to run big appliances like an air conditioner or a microwave, a 12V system has to work incredibly hard, requiring massive, expensive, and dangerous wires.

By upgrading to a 48V system, everything runs smoother, cooler, and safer. The wires are thinner, and you get way more power out of your batteries.

What You Actually Need to Buy

Building a 48V system sounds scary, but it's really just three main pieces:

  • The Battery: A 48V Lithium (LiFePO4) battery pack. This stores your power safely.
  • The Inverter: This turns battery power into normal wall-outlet power so you can plug in your laptop or fridge.
  • The Charge Controller: This takes power from the sun (your panels) and feeds it safely into your battery.
