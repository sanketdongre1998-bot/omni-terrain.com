Marine Radar Explained Simply

Don't get lost in the jargon. We break down what you actually need to know to buy the right radar for safe night boating.

Old School vs. New School Radar

Buying a radar dome for your boat used to be incredibly confusing. There are essentially two types you will hear about: Magnetron (Old School) and Solid-State (New School).

Magnetron Radars are powerful but take minutes to warm up and use a lot of battery power. Solid-State Radars turn on instantly, use hardly any power, and are much better at spotting small things close to your boat—like a kayaker or a channel marker at night.

Making it Work Together

The most important thing to know is that your new radar needs to plug into your boat's screen (the Multi-Function Display). We always recommend sticking to the same brand as your screen (like Garmin or Simrad) so you can just plug it in and hit the water safely.
