Garmin Fantom vs Simrad Halo: Solid State Doppler Radar Analysis

For decades, marine radar relied on magnetron technology—a system that required a warm-up period, emitted high levels of radiation, and struggled with short-range target resolution. Today, the high-ticket marine electronics market is entirely dominated by Solid-State Doppler Radar.

In this technical breakdown, we analyze the two apex predators of the marine radar space: The Garmin Fantom series and the Simrad Halo series. Both utilize pulse compression technology, but their approach to doppler target processing differs significantly.

1. The Power of MotionScope™ vs. VelocityTrack™

Doppler radar's primary advantage is its ability to instantly highlight moving targets based on their velocity relative to your vessel. This drastically reduces the cognitive load on the captain during low-visibility navigation.

  • Garmin Fantom (MotionScope™): Garmin's algorithm uses the Doppler effect to paint moving targets in contrasting colors. Targets moving toward you are highlighted in bright red (danger), while targets moving away are painted green (safe). It is incredibly aggressive and highly visible.
  • Simrad Halo (VelocityTrack™): Simrad takes a slightly more nuanced approach. Their color scaling is dynamic based on speed. It provides a highly detailed, softer color gradient that some captains prefer for heavy harbor traffic to avoid "screen clutter."

2. Beamwidth and Target Resolution

A radar's horizontal beamwidth dictates its ability to separate two distinct targets that are close together at a distance. The smaller the degree, the sharper the "knife edge" of the radar pulse.
