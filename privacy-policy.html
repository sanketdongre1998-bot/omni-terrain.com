Draft framework

Clear rules
before launch.

This draft outlines how OMNI TERRAIN intends to present its website and customer information. It intentionally avoids claims about systems, rights, commercial terms or processes that are not yet operating.

Information we receive 01

Keep the data map honest.

At the investor-preview stage, OMNI TERRAIN does not provide customer accounts, a live cart or a checkout. Information may be received when a visitor contacts the business by email or when the browser loads website services and third-party content.

01
Information you provide

Contact details and the contents of a message when you email the business or submit a future contact request.

02
Technical information

Basic browser, device, IP-address and usage information that may be generated when a website is hosted, secured or loaded.

03
Future commerce information

Order, delivery, payment and support information will be identified in the final policy only when live commerce is enabled.

Pre-launch note: The final live policy must be matched to the actual contact forms, analytics, payment provider, email tool, cookies and vendor services in use at that time.
How information may be used 02

Use only what the operation needs.

The future privacy program should describe actual business purposes in simple terms. These can include responding to a request, operating the site, preventing abuse, meeting legal obligations and supporting a completed customer transaction.

01
Respond and support

Use contact information to answer a product, partnership or support request.

02
Operate and protect

Maintain the website, investigate security or abuse issues and keep essential systems working.

03
Run a purchase, when live

Process orders, provide delivery updates, handle returns and maintain required business records after the store launches.

Pre-launch note: Do not add a purpose to a live privacy policy unless OMNI TERRAIN actually uses information for that purpose.
Sharing and third parties 03

Name the real service categories.

A future commercial site may rely on vendors for hosting, email, payments, analytics, shipping or customer support. The final policy should state the relevant categories and describe sharing accurately, rather than using a generic promise.

01
Service providers

Vendors may process information only as needed to host, support, secure or operate the relevant service.

02
Business changes or law

Information may be handled where required by law or as part of a lawful business transaction, subject to the final policy and applicable requirements.

03
Marketing and advertising

Any future targeted advertising, affiliate tracking or marketing technology must be reviewed and described before it is used.

Pre-launch note: The live policy should not say that information is never shared unless that is fully true across hosting, email, payments, analytics and support vendors.
Choices and requests 04

Give people a clear route.

Visitors should have a simple way to ask questions about the information associated with their contact request or future customer relationship. Applicable rights and response processes depend on the final business model and relevant law.

01
Contact route

Use support@omni-terrain.com for privacy-related questions until a dedicated privacy request workflow is active.

02
Verification

Before responding to a sensitive request, the business should use a reasonable process to confirm the requester is entitled to the information.

03
Updates

The final policy should explain how future policy changes will be posted and when they take effect.

Pre-launch note: Privacy rights, response timing and required notices must be finalized with legal review once the launch jurisdictions and data practices are known.
Activation checklist

What must be
confirmed first.

Before this page is published as a live customer policy, the final wording should reflect the actual website, data practices, payment provider, fulfillment path, customer-support workflow and applicable legal review.
