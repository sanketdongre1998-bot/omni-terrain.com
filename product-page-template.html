Preview structure
Overview section

What the product
is for.

This area gives the buyer a plain-English explanation before the detailed specifications. It should state the main purpose of the product, the customer it helps, and the real use case it supports.

01

Clear purpose

A concise explanation of the problem the product is meant to solve—not marketing noise or copied catalog wording.

02

Important limitations

The listing should surface any conditions, exclusions or setup requirements that change whether the product is right for the buyer.

03

Support path

When a question needs confirmation, the buyer can request help before they order rather than guessing from an incomplete product title.

Key details section

Details that
should be visible.

BrandShown only when the approved supplier data is available.
Part / ModelExact part number, model or SKU from the approved product source.
CategoryAutomotive, Marine, RV & Solar, plus the useful subcategory.
ConditionNew aftermarket product, unless a future listing clearly states otherwise.
What’s includedOnly confirmed package contents—no assumptions about hardware or accessories.
Important noteRelevant product-specific guidance, shown where a buyer needs it.
Listing principle: every product page should make it easier to decide, not just easier to click. Missing information is a reason to pause a listing—not a reason to hide it.
Fitment / compatibility section

Show the checks
before the cart.

Compatibility is not optional on technical products. A live OMNI TERRAIN listing will use this area to make the information that changes buying decisions visible before the buyer tries to order.

1Automotive: vehicle year, make, model, trim, engine or towing configuration when applicable.
2Marine: boat system, voltage, connection type, water use and matching equipment when applicable.
3RV & Solar: power demand, battery type, voltage, space, existing components and installation plan when applicable.
