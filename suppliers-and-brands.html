How we operate

Protect the brand.
Serve the customer.

We want a clear, compliant working relationship from the beginning. That means respecting supplier rules, keeping product information accurate and being transparent with customers.

Our supplier standard

We sell only where we are allowed to sell.

OMNI TERRAIN intends to sell products through its own website and selected U.S. ecommerce channels. Marketplace listing will only happen where the supplier or brand permits it. We do not want to create unauthorized listings, pricing problems or customer confusion.

01
Respect marketplace rulesWe ask suppliers to clearly tell us which channels, marketplaces and product lines are authorized.
02
Respect pricing policiesWe will follow written MAP, advertised-price or other brand pricing rules shared with us.
03
Use accurate product informationWe value complete titles, images, specifications, fitment details and current inventory data.
04
Support customers clearlyWe plan to communicate honestly about availability, delivery, returns and product questions.

A simple partnership path

Start small.
Build properly.

We prefer a controlled beginning: learn the supplier rules, select suitable products, build clean listings and then expand based on performance.

STEP 01

Share the basics

We review product categories, application requirements, policies, wholesale terms and available product information.

STEP 02

Confirm channels

We confirm where products may be sold, including website, eBay, Amazon or other marketplace restrictions.

STEP 03

Choose a first set

We start with suitable products that have clear use cases, strong information and a manageable fulfillment process.

STEP 04

Grow with data

We expand only after we understand customer questions, product performance, margins and supplier operations.

Helpful information to share

The clearer the start,
the better the partnership.

These are the main things we ask suppliers and brands to clarify. It helps both sides avoid problems before products go live.

Product & brand details

Useful product information helps us make better listings and answer customer questions correctly.

  • Catalog, price list, UPCs and SKUs
  • Product images, specifications and fitment information
  • Warranty and brand policy information
  • MAP or advertised-price requirements

Sales channel rules

We need written clarity about where and how products can be offered.

  • Website, eBay and Amazon authorization status
  • Restricted brands, categories or marketplace policies
  • Rules for product content, images and brand presentation
  • Any approval process required before listing

Fulfillment & returns

Clear operations help customers receive accurate information before and after an order.

  • Order cut-off, handling time and shipping options
  • Tracking process and inventory update method
  • Return process, restocking rules and defect procedures
  • Whether direct-to-customer fulfillment is available

Documentation

We want to keep records in order and meet marketplace requirements only where applicable.

  • Wholesale invoices and order records
  • Authorization or reseller documents where provided
  • Brand or marketplace compliance guidance
  • Best point of contact for follow-up questions
